**On this page**, you will find the complete worklog documenting my internship journey participating in the First Cloud Journey program.

The program is divided into two main phases. During the first 6 weeks, I focused on exploring and mastering the AWS ecosystem. From week 7 onwards, I participated in developing a real-world project named **Wakan**, taking on the role of **Team Lead & Backend Engineer** — responsible for the Lambda Orchestrator, business logic (quota, cache), and technical synthesis.

**Phase 1: Exploring and Mastering the AWS Ecosystem**
* **Week 1:** [Environment setup, VPC routing, EC2 compute, S3 storage, and Budgets](1.1-week1/)
* **Week 2:** [Broad data layer (RDS, DynamoDB, Cache) and edge delivery (CloudFront)](1.2-week2/)
* **Week 3:** [Multi-layer defense (Cognito, WAF, KMS) and multi-VPC networks](1.3-week3/)
* **Week 4:** [Automation via Lambda, IaC (CloudFormation, CDK), and FinOps mindset](1.4-week4/)
* **Week 5:** [Containers in practice (ECS, EKS), automated CI/CD pipelines and workflows](1.5-week5/)
* **Week 6:** [Microservices decoupling, Data Lake analytics, and basic AI/ML lifecycle](1.6-week6/)

**Phase 2: Practical Project Development (Wakan Project)**
* **Week 7:** [Kickoff, trip-search use-case, draft DynamoDB schema](1.7-week7/)
* **Week 8:** [API Gateway + Lambda Orchestrator skeleton, detailed DynamoDB schema](1.8-week8/)
* **Week 9:** [Orchestrator logic: tier quota, cache key, cache-before-AI](1.9-week9/)
* **Week 10:** [Debug cache hit/miss, tune TTL, full-system E2E test](1.10-week10/)
* **Week 11:** [Load test, tune Lambda memory/timeout, fix race condition](1.11-week11/)
* **Week 12:** [Technical report, demo, business-logic Q&A, final submission](1.12-week12/)
