## Notes I kept

### 1) Psychology of building software
Not generic soft-skill slides — two talks hit common failure modes:

| Problem | Speaker | What I took home |
|---------|---------|------------------|
| Procrastination | Khac Uy Pham | Often **fear of judgment / failure**, not laziness. "5-minute rule": start the smallest action to break the freeze. |
| Low confidence | Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu | Imposter syndrome & Dunning–Kruger. Confidence grows from **preparation + acting fast** ("5-second rule") before self-doubt wins. |

### 2) Practicing cloud without bill anxiety
**Huynh Thai Linh** outlined safer practice paths:
- **Cloud Quest** for structured learning tracks.
- **Floci** — open-source AWS simulator on a local machine, faster and lighter than community LocalStack for basic architecture experiments.

→ Useful for weeks 1–6: try VPC/IAM/pipelines while protecting credit.

### 3) DevOps as an iceberg
**Tran Minh Quan:** late bugs are only the tip. Under the waterline sit **manual processes** and **siloed teams**. Real DevOps is **people + process + technology**, not tools alone.

### 4) Product stories & hackathons
- **Team Khuong:** 36h LotusHacks → **UTMorpho** (AI edits UI in place); also **SynthHunter** (detect synthetic voice) and **Vortex** (hiring flow). Theme: real pain + team chemistry beat "who codes best".
- **Tran Huu Nghia:** "Tu Vi Dai Viet" on AWS (Bedrock, Lambda, DynamoDB) — codified traditional rules + personalized LLM answers, showing AI works when **rules and data can be encoded**.

---

## Personal take

The Meet Up balanced **mindset** and **practice**: half about how engineers feel, half about tools and shippable stories. For Wakan I wrote:
- Split work small and ship early instead of waiting for "perfect".
- Use cheap/local labs before spending production credit.
- Backend / frontend / AI need **contracts and stand-ups** — that is the hidden DevOps iceberg if ignored.
